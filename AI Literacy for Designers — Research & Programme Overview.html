01 — Why this matters

The gap between using AI and understanding it

Designers are already working with AI tools daily — Figma AI, Adobe Firefly, ChatGPT, generative image platforms. But tool familiarity is not the same as conceptual understanding. Without the underlying mental model, prompting becomes trial and error, and communication with developers and product leads gets vague.

Vague language creates ambiguous briefs. Ambiguous briefs create rework. This programme addresses the root cause: a shared vocabulary and a sound conceptual foundation, built specifically for visual thinkers in a design environment.

"The biggest failure mode in AI training for non-technical teams is vague vocabulary creating false confidence. The glossary is not supplementary content — it is the product."

02 — Research Findings

Six signals from the evidence on what makes AI literacy training work

01

Frame it as augmentation

AI literacy lands best when it demonstrates AI enhancing human skills — creativity, judgement, domain expertise — not replacing them. Designers are naturally primed for this frame. It's the tools story they already live.

US Dept of Labor AI Literacy Framework, Feb 2026
02

Hands-on beats lecture

Self-paced interactive learning consistently outperforms slide decks. The mechanism is immediate feedback — not content quality. People learn better when they get a response to their action now, not at the end of a session.

30% outcome improvement attributed to feedback loops alone
03

Microlearning works

Short modules embedded in workflow context drive adoption. Traditional LMS-based training sees ~20% completion. Gamified, interaction-rich platforms with short sessions regularly hit 90%+. Duration and friction are the enemy.

360Learning / EdApp: 91% vs 20% completion rates
04

Progressive disclosure

Foundational literacy first, then visible routes to go deeper. Branching paths let curious learners explore without making everyone feel gated. The core track should be completable; the deep dives should be irresistible.

DOL Framework: clear pathways from foundational to specialist
05

Language precision is the product

The single most common failure mode in AI training for non-technical teams is vague vocabulary creating false confidence. Shared definitions don't just help people talk about AI — they are the literacy.

Primary design goal: eliminate ambiguity in team communication
06

Ethics cannot be an afterthought

Most AI literacy programmes are pro-hype by default. A credible programme explicitly covers hallucination, bias, provenance, and ownership — giving learners frameworks for critical evaluation, not just caution. Scepticism is a skill.

Module 7 is structural, not a bolt-on

04 — System Architecture

04

Layers of the learning system

Self-hosted. No third-party platform. Built iteratively — one module at a time, reviewed and approved before the next begins.

Layer 1

Hub Dashboard

Home screen. Progress tracker, module map, glossary access. Visual-first navigation. Always know where you are and what's next.

Layer 2

Core Modules ×7

Visual concept explanation → worked design example → quiz with immediate feedback → badge unlock. Completable in one sitting.

Layer 3

Branching Deep-Dives

Unlocked from Module 3. Optional — never blocking. LLMs in depth, image models, agents, tool-specific guides.

Layer 4

Glossary

30–40 terms. Accessible from any module at any time. Plain English + design-world analogy per term.

.md

Core context, quiz content, glossary entries. Retrievable, version-controllable, session-portable.

.html

Interactive learner-facing deliverables. Visual-first. Self-contained — no server required.

Diagrams

SVG / Mermaid concept and system visualisations. One per module minimum.

06 — Design Principles

Seven rules for how the programme is built

01

Plain language first

No jargon without a definition. Every technical term gets plain English before it gets a name.

02

Visual before verbal

Diagrams before paragraphs. Interactive before static. Graspable before readable.

03

Design-world analogies

Every concept gets a real design parallel — genuine structural similarities, not stretched metaphors.

04

No dead ends

Branching paths are always optional, never blocking. Curiosity is rewarded, not penalised.

05

Short chunks

No wall of text. Key terms called out explicitly. Scannable before readable.

06

Immediate feedback

Quizzes give reasons, not just scores. Wrong answers teach as much as right ones.

07

Direct and warm

Not corporate. Not patronising. Direct challenge over false reassurance. Trust the audience.
